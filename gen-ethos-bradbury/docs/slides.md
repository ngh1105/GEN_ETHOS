# GEN-ETHOS Slide Deck

This file is written so you can copy it directly into Canva, Figma, or Google Slides.

Keep the deck to 7 slides. Do not overload it. One clear point per slide.

## Slide 1

Title:
GEN-ETHOS

Subtitle:
The verification and enforcement layer for corporate claims

Body:
- Companies publish claims every day
- Most claims are still self-reported
- Verification is slow
- Consequences are weak

Speaker note:
We are building a system that turns a corporate claim from marketing text into a commitment that can be audited and enforced.

## Slide 2

Title:
The Problem

Body:
- Corporate disclosures are easy to publish
- Independent verification is fragmented and delayed
- Public trust is low
- AI makes polished but unverified claims easier to generate

Bottom line:
Claims are cheap. Credibility is expensive.

Speaker note:
The core issue is not lack of reporting. The issue is lack of verifiable accountability.

## Slide 3

Title:
What GEN-ETHOS Does

Body:
1. Register claim
2. Stake escrow
3. Submit evidence
4. Run GenLayer audit
5. Record verdict
6. Apply consequence

Callout:
Claim -> audit -> consequence

Speaker note:
The important shift is that the output is not just a dashboard metric. The output changes state and creates visible consequences.

## Slide 4

Title:
Why GenLayer

Body:
- Reads public web evidence
- Supports non-deterministic AI reasoning
- Uses validator consensus on real-world audit outcomes
- Fits claim verification better than a standard deterministic contract

Speaker note:
This is exactly the kind of workflow that is hard to do cleanly on a normal chain and well suited to GenLayer.

## Slide 5

Title:
MVP Demo Flow

Body:
- Register company target
- Deposit escrow
- Request audit
- Receive verdict
- Show score and escrow change
- Review public audit history

Speaker note:
This is a complete end-to-end loop. A company makes a claim, stakes behind it, gets audited, and the result is visible in protocol state.

## Slide 6

Title:
Why This Is Bigger Than ESG

Body:
- Start with emissions and greenwashing
- Extend to supply chain claims
- Extend to vendor compliance
- Extend to impact reporting
- Extend to any trust-sensitive corporate disclosure

Tagline:
ESG is the wedge. Corporate credibility is the market.

Speaker note:
We start with a narrow pain point, but the primitive is broader: any claim that needs public verification and consequence.

## Slide 7

Title:
What Makes GEN-ETHOS Different

Body:
- Not just AI scoring
- Not just analytics
- Not just compliance reporting
- Public verdict history
- Reputation updates
- Economic enforcement through escrow

Closing line:
GEN-ETHOS makes corporate credibility programmable.

Speaker note:
Other systems evaluate. We enforce. That is the key difference.

## Design Notes

Use this visual hierarchy:

- Bold title
- One core sentence
- 3 to 6 bullets max
- One highlighted tagline or closing line

Recommended visual direction:

- high-contrast
- black / white / acid green accents
- protocol and ledger aesthetic
- simple diagrams over dense text
